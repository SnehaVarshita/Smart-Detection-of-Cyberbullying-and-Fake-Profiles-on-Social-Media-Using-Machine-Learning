About Fake Profile


A Fake Profile is an online account created with false or misleading information, often used to impersonate someone, scam users, spread misinformation, or engage in malicious activities. These profiles typically display suspicious behavior such as:

  • Using stolen or AI-generated profile pictures
  • Having incomplete or copied bios
  • Showing unusual follower/following patterns
  • Posting little or no genuine content
  • Sharing external links leading to scams or phishing sites

Detecting fake profiles early helps protect the online community from fraud, cyberbullying, identity theft, and other security threats.

Our system analyzes various factors — including profile pictures, username patterns, external links, and bio descriptions — to accurately detect potential fake profiles.

Cyber Bullying


Cyberbullying is the act of using digital platforms — such as social media, messaging apps, forums, or gaming communities — to harass, threaten, embarrass, or target another person. It can take many harmful forms, including:

  • Sending hurtful or threatening messages
  • Spreading false information or rumors
  • Posting embarrassing photos or videos without consent
  • Excluding individuals from online groups or conversations
  • Impersonating someone to damage their reputation

Cyberbullying can cause serious emotional, psychological, and social harm to individuals.

Our system monitors patterns in text, profiles, and interactions to identify and prevent cyberbullying, helping create a safer and more supportive online environment for everyone.
